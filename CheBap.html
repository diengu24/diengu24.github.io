Che Bap (Vietnamese Corn Pudding)




Ingredients:
  1. 3 large ears corn, shucked
  2. 4 cups water
  3. 2 pandan leaves, tied together and knotted (optional)
  4. 1/4 cup small tapioca pearls
  5. 2 (14-ounce) cans coconut milk
  6. 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  7. Pinch salt
  8. 2 tablespoons toasted sesame seeds
  1. Stand each ear of corn up in a bowl and, holding it sturdy, run a sharp chef's knife down the length of the ear to shave off the kernels. Set the kernels aside.
  2. Combine the corn cobs, pandan leaves (optional), and water in a pot and bring to a boil. Cover, reduce heat, and simmer for 30 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, place tapioca pearls in a small bowl, cover with cold water by 1/2 inch and let stand.
  4. Remove corn cobs and pandan leaves from the pot and discard.
  5. Add coconut milk, corn kernels, sugar, and a pinch of salt to the pot and stir to combine. Return to a boil and then simmer until corn is tender, about 10 minutes. Taste and adjust sugar and salt, if desired.
  6. Stir tapioca pearls into the pot and simmer for 2 minutes.
  7. Remove from heat to cool. Can refrigerate for up to 2 days. If it gets too thick and gloppy, thin it with water.
  8. Serve warm or cold in bowls or glasses. Garnish with sesame seeds just before serving.
