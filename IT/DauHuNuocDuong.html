Che Bap Dau Hu Nuoc Duong
Dau Hu Nuoc Duong (Tofu in Ginger Syrup)




Ingredients:
  1. 90 g brown sugar ½ cup
  2. 20 ml water ½ cup
  3. 1 pinch salt
  4. 30 g ginger thinly sliced 1 oz
  5. 1 package tofu mix 85g, 3.03oz
  6. 1.2 liter water 5 cups
  1. In a saucepan, combine the brown sugar, water, salt and sliced ginger. Stir well and bring to a boil. Then reduce the heat to low and simmer for 5 minutes. When it is still hot, the syrup is a bit watery, but after cooling down or chilled, it becomes thicker.
  2. The tofu mix package includes a large soya powder pack and a small coagulant powder pack. In a large saucepan, combine the soya powder and water. Use a whisk to whisk/stir vigorously until completely dissolved.
  3. Bring the mixture to a boil while constantly whisking. Once it boils, reduce the heat to medium low and simmer for ~3-4 minutes. Skim off the foam.
  4. In a large bowl, dissolve the coagulant powder with 2 tbsp water and tilt the bowl in a circular motion to coat the bowl with the liquid. Then pour the soya mixture to the bowl through a mesh strainer to make sure no foam or lump go into it. Cover with plastic wrap or aluminium foil and let it rest for 20 minutes or until set.
  5. To serve, use a flat ladle, gently shave thin layer of the tofu pudding and slide it into a serving bowl. (Skim off and get rid of the excess soy water in the pudding if any.) Drizzle a few tablespoons of the ginger ginger syrup on top and serve.
